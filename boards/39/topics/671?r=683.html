Refining Ground Sphere testing schedule

Added by J. Simmons about 9 years ago

Greetings everyone,

Greg and I have been talking about how to go about getting the Ground Sphere tested in light of the general lack of operational satellites broadcasting on 915 MHz. I think we worked out a pretty good plan and I wanted to share it with you. It is based around using the one operational satellite we are aware of, which only broadcasts when it is over its control station in LA. It turns out, I have a good friend from work who lives in LA and I think would be willing (and is certainly able) to test the Ground Sphere. Of course, that would require shipping the ground station to him for testing, so we need to adjust the build schedule some, as you will see below.

  • J finds out on Monday if his friend is available to help with the testing and looks at when the satellite will actually be flying over LA during the testing window
  • Move up the completion date of Prototype 1 to 10/23 (this mostly affects Aaron)
  • Ship Prototype 1 to LA on 10/23
  • J's friend in LA conducts operational tests of the Ground Sphere from 10/26 to 11/1
  • J's friend ships Prototype 1 to Tim for space tweet testing (can Prototype 1 demonstrate reception and decoding of tweets sent from the SkyCube engineering model?) on 11/1
  • Tim tests space tweet capabilities with Prototype 1 (in lab and with some separation between satellite model and Prototype 1) 11/1 - 11/8

At this point, we basically return to our current schedule, with the note that by the time Prototype 2 is ready for testing, we are assuming the two new NPS cubesats are on orbit and broadcasting so Aaron can conduct additional operational tests using Prototype 2 and the NPS satellites
